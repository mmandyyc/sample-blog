Jinius Café, Sydney: The Brunch Spot Everyone’s Talking About 🍞✨

A sun-soaked Dawes Point brunch spot with viral fig toast, Korean-influenced pastries and major slow-morning energy.

★★★★★

If you’ve been scrolling TikTok lately, chances are Jinius has already popped up on your feed—and for good reason. Tucked away in Dawes Point near the Harbour Bridge, this sun-soaked café is quickly becoming one of Sydney’s most talked-about brunch destinations.

TikTok reviewers can’t get enough of the atmosphere. Think floor-to-ceiling windows, soft natural light, and that “slow morning” energy that makes you want to linger over coffee. The space feels polished yet relaxed—perfect for dates, catch-ups, or solo brunch content creation.

Let’s talk food—the real reason people queue. The fig French toast is easily the star of the show: fluffy, cloud-like and absolutely worth ordering. The hojicha fig toast feels more elevated and artistic, with earthy notes balanced by sweetness. Even the banana bread with kombu butter sounds simple until that hit of umami changes the whole thing.

Behind the scenes, Jinius leans hard into pastry craftsmanship: airy sourdough, croissants and milk cream doughnuts, all with subtle Korean influences. Smooth cappuccinos, rich hot chocolate and house-made teas complete the picture.

One reality check: this is not a quick coffee run. Expect a wait, and expect to settle in. But if you come with time, Jinius delivers a full brunch moment that absolutely earns the hype.
